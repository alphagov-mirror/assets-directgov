Directgov - Business Link transition toolkit v3.6

Getting started with Toolkit v3.6

Directgov and Business Link are due to be replaced by GOV.UK in Autumn 2012.

We are currently working on a new set of design principles which will, in time, cover all tools & transactions surfaced on GOV.UK.

GOV.UK design principles

In the meantime, the standard Directgov and Business Link toolkits v3.5 or earlier, should not be applied to any new tools or transactions.

If you are intending to launch a new tool or transaction before Autumn 2012, contact Graham Francis on +44 (0)7798 926 978 or by email for advice on branding, and to access the revised 'transition' toolkit.

Email Graham Francis, Transition team

  • if you feel you must launch a new tool or transaction on the Directgov or Business Link domain, you should use the debranded transition toolkit, version 3.6 which we have put together (eg rather than the standard Directgov toolkit, version 3.5, which is no longer available)
  • to access the debranded toolkit, first raise a proposition form for your new service with the transformation team, in the normal way
  • if proposition directors accept the proposition, they will hand you over to the Directgov transition team to manage delivery - the transition team will provide you with the toolkit and any advice your require
  • if you believe that implementation of the debranded toolkit would incur significant cost and/or delay to your programme, then you should raise this with the transition team manager (who will take the position back to prop directors)

What is the toolkit?

The toolkit is a resource for designers and developers involved in the delivery of government services.

What are tools and transactions?

Tools and transactions are standalone applications, hosted and run outside of the Directgov, Business Link and GOV.UK core infrastructure.
